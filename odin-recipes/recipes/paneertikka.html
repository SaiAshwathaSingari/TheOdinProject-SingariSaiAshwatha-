Paneer Tikka Recipe

Description

Paneer Tikka is a popular Indian appetizer made by marinating cubes of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) in a spiced yogurt mixture and grilling them to perfection. To prepare, start by mixing plain yogurt with spices such as cumin, coriander, garam masala, turmeric, and red chili powder. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and lemon juice to the marinade for an extra burst of flavor. Toss cubes of paneer in the marinade and let them absorb the spices for at least 30 minutes. Skewer the marinated paneer along with vegetables like onions, bell peppers, and tomatoes. Grill or bake until the paneer is golden and slightly charred on the edges. Serve with mint chutney and lemon wedges for a delicious and smoky appetizer. Paneer Tikka is a vegetarian favorite, perfect for barbecues, parties, or as a starter to an Indian meal.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup butter
  • 1 pound paneer, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 1 medium green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 medium jalapeno peppers, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ground cashews
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 (16 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 pint half-and-half
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste

Steps

  1. Melt butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add paneer; cook and stir until golden, about 5 minutes. Add onions, bell pepper, jalapeños, ground cashews, garlic paste, ginger paste, cayenne pepper, cumin, coriander, and garam masala; cook and stir until well combined and fragrant, about 1 minute.
  2. Mix tomato sauce, half-and-half, and salt into paneer mixture; simmer until thickened, about 30 minutes.
